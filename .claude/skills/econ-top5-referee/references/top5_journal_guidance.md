# Top 5 Economics Journal Referee Guidance

Use this reference when `econ-top5-referee` must tailor reports to AER, QJE, RES/REStud, JPE, or ECTA/Econometrica. Default to AER if the user does not specify a journal.

## Common Top 5 Standard

For all Top 5 journals, evaluate both validity and importance. A paper must be technically credible, novel relative to the literature, clear enough for its intended economics audience, and important enough for the target journal's scarce space. Do not treat a correct but incremental paper as sufficient for a Top 5 recommendation. Do not turn the referee into a coauthor: separate essential publishability blockers from optional improvements.

Use Berk, Harvey, and Hirshleifer (JEP 2017) as the cross-journal referee standard: advise the editor decisively, identify what blocks publication, avoid vague disbelief, avoid excessive robustness demands, and label suggestions as suggestions.

## Journal Aliases

- AER: American Economic Review
- QJE: Quarterly Journal of Economics
- RES, REStud, ReStud: Review of Economic Studies
- JPE: Journal of Political Economy
- ECTA, Econometrica: Econometrica

## AER

Public guidance:

- AER uses single-blind review: authors are known to referees, referees remain anonymous to authors.
- The editor assigns a paper to a coeditor or handles it as coeditor. The assigned coeditor oversees review and makes the publication decision.
- AER may return manuscripts without referee review based on expected probability of meeting AER standards, breadth of topic, and interest to the AER audience.
- Before a first revise-and-resubmit decision, the coeditor consults a second coeditor.
- Conditionally accepted papers undergo AEA Data Editor review for data/code availability and data legality.
- Reviewer recommendations should be based on whether conclusions are robust, credible, and worthy of publication, not on disclosure statements alone.

Referee emphasis:

- General-interest economics contribution and broad AER audience.
- Robustness, credibility, and worthiness of publication.
- Breadth beyond a narrow field-journal result.
- Data/code, disclosure, IRB, RCT registration, and AI-use flags when relevant.

## QJE

Public guidance:

- QJE covers all aspects of economics, from its traditional microtheory emphasis to empirical and theoretical macroeconomics.
- It is a high-impact journal with a rigorous editorial process and fair peer review.
- All submissions are initially reviewed by the Editor and Associates. Papers may be rejected without peer review if not high enough priority or not relevant to QJE.
- Manuscripts not desk rejected are usually sent to two independent reviewers. Decisions use reviewer feedback and editorial judgment; statistical review may be used.
- QJE has adopted the AER data availability policy for empirical, simulation, and experimental work.

Referee emphasis:

- Priority, importance, and high-impact contribution for a broad economics readership.
- Whether the paper is more than a technically sound field contribution.
- Clarity of economic question and contribution early in the paper.
- Replicability and documentation under AER-style data policy when relevant.

## RES / REStud

Public guidance:

- REStud is one of the core Top 5 economics journals and aims to encourage research in theoretical and applied economics, especially by young economists.
- It has a reputation for path-breaking papers in theoretical and applied economics.
- Editors aim for an efficient, high-quality review process. For papers sent to full review, authors should receive careful reports and feedback.
- REStud has historically been strong in microeconomics, economic theory, and econometrics while increasing coverage of macroeconomics, applied econometrics, finance, experimental economics, and political economics.
- Public editor reports emphasize thoughtful, thorough, professional referee reports within deadlines.

Referee emphasis:

- Path-breaking theoretical or applied contribution.
- Strength in theory, micro, and econometrics, while accepting broader economics fields.
- Constructive but decisive feedback, especially useful for promising papers and emerging authors.
- Efficient review: identify the few essential issues rather than a sprawling revision agenda.

## JPE

Public guidance:

- Public standalone referee instructions are less visible than for AER or Econometrica; use official policy plus Top 5 norms.
- JPE's data and code policy requires clearly documented and readily available data and code for replication, subject to stated exemptions.
- Conditionally accepted empirical, experimental, simulation, and numerical papers undergo reproducibility checks. Publication is conditional on a positive outcome.
- Exemption requests for data/code restrictions should be stated when the paper is first submitted; later exceptions are not considered.
- The handling editor decides whether a paper with such restrictions should be reviewed.

Referee emphasis:

- Major contribution to economic theory, empirical economics, political economy, macro, micro, or related fields at JPE level.
- Depth, originality, and intellectual durability, not just broad topical appeal.
- Strong attention to data/code feasibility, computational reproducibility, and stated exemptions.
- If public JPE-specific reviewer rules are unavailable, state that the report applies Top 5 economics and JPE data/reproducibility standards rather than inventing journal instructions.

## ECTA / Econometrica

Public guidance:

- Econometrica asks referees to respond within six weeks or decline if they cannot meet the deadline.
- Referees should disclose if they have reviewed the paper before.
- Reports should be professional and generous with praise; frank assessments belong in the confidential letter to the coeditor.
- Econometrica encourages a three-part report: Summary, Essential Points, and Suggestions.
- Essential Points should explain what is necessary to meet the bar for revise-and-resubmit or what makes the paper unpublishable. The critical list should generally include no more than three items and preferably fewer.
- Reports should not be long; two or three pages is enough for most papers.
- Econometrica editorial policy stresses wide audience interest for Econometric Society members. A theoretical paper need not have an application if insightful; empirical/applied work can be important without a methodological contribution. Non-specialists should understand the main message and importance.

Referee emphasis:

- Theoretical, econometric, methodological, or economically important contribution that speaks beyond a narrow subfield.
- Proof correctness, assumptions, identification, model logic, and generality where relevant.
- A short Essential Points list; avoid bloated requests and unnecessary appendices.
- Clear distinction between essential revisions and optional suggestions.

## Report Format by Journal

Use the user's requested format if specified. Otherwise:

- AER, QJE, RES, JPE: use the standard Top 5 referee package: confidential cover letter plus report to authors, with sections for summary, importance, fatal blockers, fixable blockers, and optional suggestions.
- ECTA: use `Summary`, `Essential Points`, and `Suggestions` in the report to authors, plus a confidential letter to the coeditor.
